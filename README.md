## 1. Executive Overview

This guide is your career operating system — a living, milestone-driven blueprint to transition from a seasoned Principal Engineer with deep DevSecOps and embedded systems expertise into a world-class AI Architect and eventually an AI Research Scientist. It is specifically crafted for the 2026 AI landscape, where the convergence of LLMs, agentic systems, and AI infrastructure has created entirely new career paths that did not exist five years ago. ### Goal
Conduct an honest self-assessment of current strengths and gaps. Your 15+ years in DevSecOps, embedded systems, CI/CD, and Kubernetes represent extraordinary leverage for an AI transition — far more than a fresh ML graduate carries. ## 6. Stage 3 — Senior AI Architect / AI Platform Lead

### Goal
Lead the design and delivery of enterprise-scale AI platforms, govern multi-model ecosystems, and create the organizational AI operating model. At this stage, you become the person others look to for both the strategic direction and the technical depth of AI infrastructure. [web:21]

## 7. Stage 4 — Applied AI Research Scientist

### Goal
Transition from building AI systems to advancing them. The Applied AI Research Scientist operates at the boundary between engineering and academia — conducting systematic experiments, reproducing landmark papers, and publishing original findings within applied contexts. [web:11]

## 8. Stage 5 — Full AI Research Scientist

### Goal
Conduct original, publishable research that advances the state of the art in your chosen specialization. A Full Research Scientist owns a research agenda, manages research projects independently, publishes at top-tier venues (NeurIPS, ICML, ICLR, ACL, CVPR), and contributes meaningfully to the global AI research community. [web:3]

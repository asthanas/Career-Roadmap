## 34. Career Strategy Notes

### Building Influence as a Principal Engineer
The fastest path to influence in 2026 is through written artifacts — RFCs, ADRs, and Architecture Decision Records that become canonical references within your organization. [web:1] Every time you write a well-reasoned RFC that gets adopted, you create a permanent signal of your technical authority that no job title can replace. Write at least one RFC per quarter that proposes a forward-looking AI or platform architecture change.

### Writing RFCs and Architecture Decision Records (ADRs)
A strong RFC has the following structure: context, decision drivers, considered options, decision outcome, consequences, and links to prior ADRs. Keep ADRs short and store them in Markdown format. For AI-specific ADRs, include model selection rationale, evaluation metrics, fallback strategy, cost model, security threat model, and observability plan.

### Leading Architecture Review Boards (ARBs)
An effective Architecture Review Board is a learning forum, not a gate. Structure ARB sessions around proposal, Q&A, principles review, decision, and documented ADR outcome. For AI ARBs, add hallucination risk, data privacy, model drift, LLMOps readiness, and governance alignment.

### Transitioning from Engineer → Architect → Researcher
The Engineer-to-Architect transition is primarily a scope expansion. The Architect-to-Researcher transition is a mode shift from optimizing known solutions to discovering unknown ones. Treat your M.Tech thesis as your first real research contribution and choose a topic that bridges your strengths with a genuine research gap.

### Measuring and Communicating Business Impact
Every AI initiative should be anchored to a business metric, not just a technical outcome. Baseline the current metric, state your hypothesis, measure the outcome, and translate the result into dollars, hours, or risk reduction. [web:1]

### Preparing for Promotion at Each Stage
| Stage Transition | Primary Signal Needed | Secondary Signal |
|---|---|---|
| Principal → Senior Principal | Org-wide influence artifact | External visibility |
| Senior Principal → AI Architect | Production AI system with measured impact | Cert and adoption |
| AI Architect → Senior AI Architect | Platform others use | Mentorship and talk |
| Senior AI Architect → Applied Researcher | Publication or preprint | University collaboration |
| Applied → Full Researcher | Main-track conference paper | Reviewer role and citations |

### Building a Research Portfolio
Your research portfolio should include GitHub repos, arXiv preprints, blog posts, talks, and citations. Start building it during Stage 1 so the compounding signal is visible by the time you apply to labs or publications.

### Getting into Top AI Labs Without a PhD
It is entirely possible to join an industry research lab without a PhD in 2026, particularly in applied research and research engineering roles. [web:2] Build a strong public research portfolio, publish workshop papers, contribute to open source, network with researchers, and apply for industry-track roles. Your blend of Principal Engineer depth, M.Tech, and research output is a strong differentiator.
